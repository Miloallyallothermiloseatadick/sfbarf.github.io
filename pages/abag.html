ABAG

What is ABAG?

Wikipedia says that the Association of Bay Area Governments (ABAG) is a regional planning agency incorporating various local governments in the San Francisco Bay Area in California. It deals with land use, housing, environmental quality, and economic development. Non-profit organizations as well as governmental organizations can be members. All nine counties and 101 cities within the Bay Area are voluntary members of ABAG.

MTC meeting

On Friday, May 8, several developers made a presentation on the housing crisis in the bay. This is a summary of their slides, available here, as well as drawing from other sources. Here's their take.

Essentially, the Bay Area is a huge draw to professionals both fresh out of school as well as looking for something bigger. We have good weather, good jobs, and the economy is red hot.

The millenial generation is flocking to the Bay Area. The tech natives are hungry, smart, and they're snapping up all the housing.

Unfortunately, the current bureaucratic environment is ill equipped to deal with this massive influx. The lead time for new development is long. By the time new housing is added, many long-time residents find themselves pushed out by the spry upstarts.

Issues

Several vectors exist to drop show stopper bombs on the housing development process. "Concerned" residents as well as outright nimbyists can invoke environmental reviews to gum up the works. In several jurisducionts, ballot measures have been created to limit development at a time when more housing is needed than ever. Established communities, counterintuitively, resist new development efforts. This neither stems the tide of incoming professionals nor houses those already here. It does drive out the poor. Besides this, many coastal communities have adopted policies that limit growth.
