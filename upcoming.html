What is this?

Have you ever thought to yourself, "I wish the Bay Area could build more housing units"?
US TOO. As it turns out, every week there are opportunities for you, dear member of the public, to weigh in on whether specific projects should be built. Join our googlegroup to be alerted of opportunities to tell decision makers all over the Bay that it's time to get going with housing production.
"Uhh, can't I just vote on something?" Sure, put your contact info and street address in the form below. We will email you when it is time to vote. SIGN UP NO MATTER WHERE YOU LIVE, WHETHER OR NOT YOU ALREADY REGISTERED TO VOTE. Elections happen everywhere, and you have until October to register.

Foster City: Pilgrim Triton Phase C Hearing

May 18, 5pm – 6pm

Foster City Council Chambers, 620 Foster City Boulevard, Foster City

Sares Regis Group is developing Phase C of the Pilgrim Triton Master Plan, which would replace the existing 172,943 sf of commercial uses/17 residential homes with 65 to 70 for-sale townhomes. Foster City City Council is scheduled to hear the proposal the evening of May 18. In the meantime, there is opposition who are also calling for a moratorium similar to those that were proposed in Walnut Creek and Emeryville. Monitoring.

SFHAC Affordable Housing Bike Tour

May 21, 10am – 1pm

Have you ever wondered what it takes to build an affordable housing development? San Francisco, a city known recently for skyrocketing rents, has actually produced a significant amount of subsidized, or below-market-rate, housing. To learn about the City’s more recent projects, we’re pedaling to four different sites – in SoMa, the Tenderloin and North Beach – where the developer and/or architect will talk about the story behind the building and how it came to be. This tour is in conjunction with the Emerging Leaders Peer Network and the San Francisco Bicycle Coalition. #SFHACTours
